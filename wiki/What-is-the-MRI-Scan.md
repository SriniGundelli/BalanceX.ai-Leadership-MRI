# What is the Leadership MRI Scan?

## Overview

The **Leadership MRI Scan** is a same-day intelligence diagnostic for executive hiring. Rather than launching straight into a full search, we run an "MRI" of the role and the market — giving you an objective, data-led view before you commit time, budget, or internal attention.

## The Core Problem

Executive hiring is broken at the **front end**:

### Common Mistakes
- **Vague role definitions** — "We need a strong CRO" without clarity on what "strong" means in your context
- **Disconnected salary bands** — Offering $150-200K for a role that commands $250K+ in the market
- **Misunderstood talent pools** — Thinking top talent is scarce when it's actually abundant (or vice versa)
- **Hidden misalignment** — Founder, CEO, and board disagree on what success looks like

These failures don't happen *during* the search. They happen *before* it starts.

## Why The MRI Works

Instead of guessing, we map the market:

- 🔍 **Role clarity** — What does this role actually need to do to succeed *in your company*?
- 🗺️ **Market reality** — Where does credible talent *actually* sit, and what do they cost?
- 💼 **Competitive positioning** — How do your package, growth path, and culture stack up?
- 🚩 **Risk intelligence** — Where will this search be hard, and why?

This is diagnostic work. You get clarity. You make better decisions. Your search moves faster.

## When to Use the MRI

**Use it when:**
- You're hiring a C-suite or mission-critical leadership role
- Internal misalignment exists on what the role should be
- You're entering a new sector or geography
- A previous search stalled or failed
- You need investor/board confidence in your hiring strategy

**Don't use it if:**
- You're confident in your role definition and market knowledge
- You're hiring for an operational role (CFO, VP Eng) where standards are clear
- You're running a routine backfill

## What You'll Receive

### 1. Role Clarity & Success Profile
A sharpened definition of what success looks like in *your* organization, grounded in market benchmarks. Includes:
- Role scope and key accountabilities
- Success criteria (quantitative and qualitative)
- Non-negotiables vs. nice-to-haves
- Typical background/experience range for credible candidates

### 2. Market Map & Talent Signal
Intelligence on *where* credible talent sits and *how to reach* them:
- Total addressable talent pool (realistic sizing)
- Where talent concentrates (company types, geographies, backgrounds)
- Incumbent analysis (who's doing this role well, and why)
- Likely candidates and how to source them
- Shortlist shape and hiring timeline

### 3. Compensation & Market Competitiveness
Directional guidance on:
- Base salary expectations (25th, 50th, 75th percentile)
- Bonus and variable compensation norms
- Equity packages and vesting
- Total compensation positioning vs. market

### 4. Risk Flags & Search Strategy
Where this hire will be hard and how to mitigate:
- Talent scarcity signals
- Compensation misalignment risks
- Typical time-to-hire for this role
- Recommended sourcing channels and process design
- Cultural fit and integration challenges to monitor
