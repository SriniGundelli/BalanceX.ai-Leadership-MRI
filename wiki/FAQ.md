# FAQ

## The MRI Scan — Basics

**Q: What exactly is the Leadership MRI Scan?**

A: It's a same-day intelligence diagnostic for executive hiring. You have a 20-minute briefing call where we unpack your role, context, and success criteria. We then spend 4-6 hours running market research using AI-enabled tools and proprietary databases. Finally, we do a 20-30 minute read-out call where we walk through findings and answer questions.

Output: A written brief covering role clarity, market map, compensation analysis, and risk flags. Nothing fancy — just actionable insights.

---

**Q: How is the MRI different from a full search?**

A: The MRI is diagnostic. We're answering: *Do we understand the role? Is the market favorable? Are we positioned competitively?*

A full search is execution. We're sourcing candidates, screening them, and vetting them for you.

Most people should do an MRI first. It saves time, money, and headache later. You either get clarity and confidence to move forward, or you learn something that changes your approach entirely.

---

**Q: How long does it take?**

A: **Standard:** Same-day delivery (briefing 9 AM → research 10 AM–4 PM → read-out 4:30 PM).

**Next-day:** If you need more research time, we can do Day 1 briefing + evening research, Day 2 morning research + afternoon read-out.

**Custom:** Need a different timeline? Let's work it out.

---

**Q: How much does it cost?**

A: **$2,500–$5,000** depending on role complexity and market difficulty.

Includes scoping call, full research, read-out call, and one round of clarifications.

## The Process

**Q: What do I need to prepare?**

A: Not much. During the briefing call, we'll ask:
- Role title, reporting line, and key accountabilities
- Success metrics (how you'll know this hire is working)
- Hiring timeline and constraints
- Budget and culture fit expectations
- Any previous hiring attempts and lessons learned

Have those in mind, and we're good to go.

---

**Q: Can I do the briefing call at a different time?**

A: Absolutely. The briefing call can happen whenever it works for you — morning, afternoon, next day, whenever. Research phase starts after the call.

---

**Q: What if I can't make time for a read-out call same day?**

A: No problem. We can schedule the read-out for whenever works. Research findings don't change — we can talk through them whenever you're available.

---

**Q: Can I involve my hiring manager, board member, or team in the briefing?**

A: Yes. In fact, we often recommend it if there's internal misalignment. The briefing becomes a working session where everyone's on the same page about the role and what success looks like.

---

**Q: How is the research conducted?**

A: We use:
- 🤖 **AI-enabled market analysis** (Claude) for real-time research and signal processing
- 📊 **Proprietary databases** and tools for executive movement, compensation benchmarks, talent flow
- 🔗 **Network intelligence** from recruiters, industry contacts, and our network
- 📈 **Historical data** on hiring trends, time-to-hire, success rates

We're not doing random LinkedIn scrolling. This is systematic market mapping using tools that actually work.

---

**Q: Are my findings confidential?**

A: 100%. Everything is treated as confidential. We don't share findings with anyone — not recruiters, not networks, not our website. After the engagement, everything is deleted unless you ask us to keep it.

## Next Steps

**Q: What happens after the read-out call?**

A: Three options:

1. **Run the search yourself** — You now have a clear brief, talent map, and sourcing strategy. Brief your recruiting team and get to work.

2. **Full retained search** — We take the lead finding and vetting candidates. We source, screen, coordinate interviews, and manage the process end-to-end. Pricing is usually 20% of first-year salary.

3. **Advisory mode** — You run the search with your team. We're in the background advising on candidate evaluation, offer strategy, and onboarding.

---

**Q: If I want to do a full search with you, when do we start?**

A: Immediately after the read-out call, if you want. Most people like to have the MRI findings for a few days, think about it, then decide. Either way works.

---

**Q: What's your placement rate?**

A: We focus on quality over volume. If we take on a full search, we're committed to finding someone who succeeds in your organization. We won't flood you with mediocre candidates or push a bad fit because we need to close a deal.

Most of our full searches close within 45-90 days depending on role difficulty. Placement rate is high (we rarely have to walk away), but we'll tell you upfront if the market is tough or the role is unfillable.

---

**Q: Can you help with offer strategy and negotiation?**

A: Yes. In fact, we usually do. Part of the MRI includes compensation positioning. If you move to a full search or advisory model, we'll help you think through offer level, equity, signing bonus, and how to position to different candidate types.

We can also coach you on negotiation — what to do if a candidate asks for more, how to handle competing offers, etc.

## Pricing & Investment

**Q: Is $2,500–$5,000 worth it?**

A: Good questions to ask yourself:
- Is this role mission-critical to my business?
- Have we struggled to hire for roles like this before?
- Would $2,500–5,000 of clarity now save me 6 months of search time?
- Do I have internal misalignment on what this role should be?

If you answered "yes" to 2+ of those, the MRI is probably worth it.

For context: a single bad hire (6-month search + 3-month onboarding + eventual replacement) costs 150K–300K+ in lost time, salary, and opportunity cost. $5K of upfront clarity is usually a bargain.

---

**Q: What if the MRI shows we shouldn't hire?**

A: That's valuable. Sometimes the market intelligence shows that the role is unfillable, the price is unjustifiable, or timing is wrong.

If that's the case, we'll tell you. And you'll have saved months by learning it now rather than discovering it during a failed search.
